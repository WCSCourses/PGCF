At a glance

A framework designed for real-world use

The PGCF is intended as an evolving, flexible framework rather than a prescriptive checklist. It helps users select the most relevant domains and competencies for their setting, then apply them to curriculum review, training design, self-assessment, or workforce development.

Breadth across the pipeline

Covers the continuum from genomes and microbiology to sample preparation, data generation, analysis, interpretation, and decision-making.

Depth through structured progression

Each topic includes six Bloom-aligned competency statements, enabling progression from foundational understanding to advanced evaluation and creation.

Cross-cutting implementation skills

Includes ethics, communication, data science, learning and training, and capacity development to support adoption beyond technical workflows alone.

Framework structure

Ten domains covering technical, applied, and cross-cutting capability

The framework organises 60 topics into domains that can be used together or selected according to role, course, or institutional need.

Domain 1

Genomes and Microbiology

Foundational biology, pathogen diversity, microbiology concepts, and the scientific context needed to interpret genomic information.

Domain 2

Sample Preparation and Processing

Specimen collection, handling, pre-analytical considerations, and preparation steps that underpin reliable downstream analysis.

Domain 3

Sample Analysis and Data Generation

Sequencing technologies, laboratory workflows, quality control, and generation of usable pathogen genomics data.

Domain 4

Genomic Data Workflows, Analysis and Integration

Core bioinformatics workflows, processing pipelines, integration of multiple data types, and reproducible analytical practice.

Domain 5

Data Interpretation

Interpretation of genomic outputs, contextualisation with epidemiological evidence, and translation into meaningful conclusions.

Domain 6

Clinical and Public Health Decision-Making

Application of pathogen genomics to surveillance, outbreak response, infection control, and decision support in health systems.

Domain 7

Data Science

Data stewardship, management, visualisation, ethics, governance, and computational practices that support trustworthy use of data.

Domain 8

Integrated Skills

Communication, interdisciplinary collaboration, leadership, and professional capabilities needed to embed genomics into practice.

Domain 9

Learning and Training

Teaching, facilitation, mentoring, and support for developing learning pathways in pathogen genomics.

Domain 10

Capacity Development and Programme Management

Planning, implementation, evaluation, coordination, and long-term development of programmes that strengthen genomics capability at institutional or national level.

How to use it

From gap analysis to workforce planning

The user guide describes several flexible ways to apply the PGCF. Users can work at the level of topics, competencies, courses, job roles, or organisational capability planning.

Gap analysis

Review an existing curriculum or programme to see which topics are fully covered, partially covered, or missing.

Curriculum design

Select relevant domains and competencies, define learning outcomes, and match teaching and assessment methods to Bloom’s levels.

Module mapping

Align course modules and learning outcomes directly to PGCF competencies for transparent coverage and progression.

Proficiency profiles

Use structured self or supervisor review to compare current and expected levels, then identify training priorities.

Who is it for?

The framework is especially useful for educators, training developers, curriculum designers, workforce planners, public health agencies, and learners working across pathogen genomics.

  • Laboratory scientists and biomedical scientists
  • Bioinformaticians and data specialists
  • Epidemiologists and public health teams
  • Clinical scientists, consultants, and researchers
